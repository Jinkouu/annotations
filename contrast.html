ANNOTATIONS IN NARRATIVE VISUALIZATION

This is a framework for annotation recommendations for different audiences in narrative visualization. By Jonathan Nguyen

Recommendations

1. Low Contrast

Screenshot - 1

U.S. Gun Deaths In...

In this example, the narrative visualisation employs a modern design approach by using a low-contrast visual scheme with thin grey text on a black background (see screenshot - 1). This creates a visually appealing experience that targets younger audiences more accustomed to a "dark mode" design aesthetic. The low-contrast choice aims to avoid distracting from the main visualisation, only providing additional explanations when needed. However, it's important to consider that this design choice poses challenges for an older demographic due to age-related visual imapirments.

Recommendation

Use only for a young adult audience

Use to allow for more exploration before explanations

DO NOT use for audience with reduced eyesight and visual impairments

2. Medium Contrast

Screenshot - 2

Wine & Math

The Pudding is a website that cater towards a primarily 18-24 year old demographic (Similar Web)

In this example it uses a medium contrast within the annotation, utilising a light brown background with dark grey text (see screenshot - 2). As the audience is primarily younger adults, the medium contrast sets an aesthetic appeal and rustic tone for the overall narrative visualisation. It also balances the levels of contrast, maintaining good readibility whilst also increasing the visual appeal. Additionally, for prolonged periods of exploration, medium-contrast may be more beneficial towards reducing eye strain when compared to a high contrasting scheme such as black text on a white background. Whilst the content can also be viewed by seniors due to its good readibility, it is primarily aimmed towards younger adults as seniors may experience limitations with regards to reduced contrast sensitivity and personal preferences.

Recommendation

Primarily use to target a younger adult audience, with other demographics being secondary

Use for informal/casual content

Consider higher contrast for audiences with visual impairments

In this example it uses a high contrast within the annotation, utilising black text on a white background (see screenshot - 3). As the content is primarily aimed at all audiences with more considerations towards older users, high contrast allows for optimal readibility, making text stand out clearly against the background. Additionally, it is generally more universally accessible and likely to be preferred by those with visual impairments.

Recommendation

Use when targeting a wider audience

Must use towards senior audiences and those with visual impairments
