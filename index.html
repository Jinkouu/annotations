ANNOTATIONS IN NARRATIVE VISUALIZATION

This is a framework for annotation recommendations for different audiences in narrative visualization. By Jonathan Nguyen

In what ways are annotations integrated into narrative visualization?

Narrative visualization otherwise known as data-stoytelling, is an important communication medium that is used to spread information such as COVID-19 advice. Audience is not given enough thought when developing narrative visualization, which is problematic, especially when it is used to convey important information.

In this work I study one element of narrative visualization to find out how it can be better used for a specific audience. I have chosen annotations because they are very important when explaining information to readers and vary greatly between narrative visualization. For the purposes of this work, annotations are any text that explains information in a narrative visualization.

Annotation Design Overview

The following list contains a comprehensive overview that explores various considerations behind effective annotation usage within narrative visualisations. Each consideration contains a hyperlink to their respective pages, showcasing narrative visualisation annotations that i have found in real-world example. For each example, i have recommended a target demographic.

Consideration Additional Information
Font & Font Size
  • Tailoring font sizes is essential to enhance the accessibility of annotations towards specific demographics. For example, larger font sizes caters to the needs of seniors, ensuring greater readability and comprehension.
  • Font choice is crucial in resonating towards a specific audience. Cursive fonts may appeal more to a younger demographic, but may not be as effective for senior, therefore, selecting fonts that align with the preferences of the intended audience is vital for effective communication.
Contrast
  • High contrasting colours improves the accessibility for individuals with visual impairments such as colour-blind readers and senior's eyesight. This is important as it ensures the content remains clear and recognisable to a wider audience.
  • High contrast also contributes to the visual aesthetics of annotations. By employing a visually engaging and pleasing colour scheme, the overall aesthetic appeal of the content is increased, thereby making a more immersive and enjoyable experience.
  • An additional factor is that colour schemes depend on the target demographic, for example, lower contrast schemes such as blue and yellow may appear acceptable when targeting a younger audience. It is therefore important to consider the context in determining the most appropriate colour palette.
Interactive elements
  • Utilising interactive elements is a powerful tool in boosting engagement amongst specific demographics, particularly, younger adults and children. This is due to the dynamic nature of interactive features and its ability to capture and encourage participation and attention.
  • There is a balance to which an excess of interactive elements can easily overwhelm users. It is crucial to take into account clutter, confusion, and target audience when designing a user-friendly annotation model.
  • Interactive elements innately contain the ability to branch into data exploration. In doing so, users gain access to additional exploration through hands-on interaction and exploration into the data, thereby creating a more immersive and informative experience.
Clickable area size
  • Clickable area sizes are an important consideration into targeting specific demographics and improving interactions.
    • For example, in increasing the size of the clickable area, accessibility is enhanced amongst audiences with impaired or reduced motor skills, such as children and seniors.
    • Conversely, smaller clickable area sizes may appear less intrusive for younger adults and therefore does not disrupt or ruin the immersive experience towards this demographic.
Transitions and Animations
  • Smooth animations and visual feedback is an important factor when utilising transitions and animations, especially when creating on-click and hover annotations as it helps ensure a seamless experience that enhances user satisfaction and immersion.
  • Poor animation implementation can disrupt the immersion of the content and become jarring when elements suddenly appear or disappear.
  • Usage of transitions and animations has the potential to increase engagemenet and interest when targeting specific demographics. When applied appropriately to a younger audience, dynamic visual elements capture greater attention, thereby contributing towards a more engaging and captivating experience.
  • Transitions and animations serve as effective tools to guide the user's focus from the data to the annotation. This movement from one element to another enhances the storytelling aspect of narrative visualisation and helps aid effective communication towards specific demographics.
Navigation Options
  • Offering different navigation options enable specific demographics to explore content based on their knowledge in the area and user preferences.
    • For example, senior audiences benefit from clearly stated navigation options such as labels that "X" and "BACK" when annotations appear. This approach reduces confusion and provides an intuitive method for senior users to navigate more effectively through the annotations.
    • Conversely, younger audience who are more familiar with interactive navigation design may require less visible annotation navigation such as click or swipe navigation, allowing for an interaction method more aligned towards their interaction patterns.
Tutorial & Help Options
  • Access towards tutorials serves as a valuable tool in reducing the learning curve for visualisation reading, particularly when interpreting and navigating through unconventional or complex graphs. This feature benefits specific demographics more than others and depends more on the intuition built by the target demographic.
    • Senior audiences that may not have extensive experiences with interactive visualisation, often require and benefit greatly from additional support to get started. By providing clear help options, the guided assistance expands the perceived functionality of the visualisation and increases user satisfaction.
    • Younger audiences typically approach data with a more adaptive mindset and may require less instructions or help, especially when navigating through familiar elements such as timelines. Therefore, it is important to consider the audiences built-up intuition for quicker engagement without excessive guidance.
Universal & Consistent Annotation Indicators
  • Universal and consistent annotation indicators decreases confusion amongst users. By utilising easily recognisable symbols, a shared understanding is created, reducing the potential for misinterpretation and confusion.
  • Usage of universal icons with clear physical associations enhances the intuitive nature of interaction and comprehension. For example, associating a magnifying glass with a method of searching, or a help option with a "?" makes the connection between the symbol and its corresponding more natural and universally understood.
  • Inclusion of universal and consistent icons serve as a powerful means to enhance visual communication as icons provide a secondary visual language that offers additional content and associations towards specific ideas.
Device Compatibility
  • Annotation design requires complex programming that stays consistent throughout different device types and platforms. This ensures seamless functionality that is consistent throughout all means of access the narrative visualisations.
    • For example, recognising that hover annotations do not work on mobile devices and an appropriate substitute is required is essential in maintaining the same quality and experience against all devices.
    • Challenges related to positioning, interactive elements, and responsiveness may appear when utilising different devices (e.g., from a computer to a mobile device). It is vital to take this into consideration and address these discrepancies as it ensures that the annotations appear as intended, maintaining a consistent user experience.
  • Optimising annotations for different devices significantly broadens the reach of the visualisation, and by catering to different screen sizes and processing powers, the visualisation becomes more accessible, enhancing its overall impact on different demographics.
Embedded Hyperlinks
  • - Hyperlinks serve as a natural means of further exploration, but requires a target demographic in mind when implemented.
    • For younger adults, hyperlinks enable for a deeper exploration into the content and the ability to access additional explanations. This aligns with their familiar digital navigational patterns and supports a more intuitive and supportive user experience.
    • However, for seniors and children, hyperlinks may introduce confusion, particularly when they lead to external websites with different layouts. Careful consideration is therefore vital in balancing the benefits of data exploration and the potential challenge of being overwhelming and overloading.
  • Embedded hyperlinks plays a key role in reducing the visual clutter within the narrative and provide an additional, unobtrusive way for users to explore data and learn from context without overwhelming the main visualisation.
  • Annotations that provide direct access to external sources of information enhances the transparency and credibility of the data source, thereby fostering a sense of trust and authenticity in the presented statistics.
Multimedia
  • Incorporation of multimedia, such as video clips and GIFs, enables a method of dual channel communication. In doing so, not only is the effectiveness of communication enhanced, it also contributes towards an improved recall and retention of the information presented.
  • The visual engagement provided by multimedia elements boosts user engagement as well as user enjoyment. This increased engagement captures both the attention of the user as well as increases the memorability of the content due to visual stimuli's ability to leave a lasting impression on the user
Gamification
  • Gamification plays an effective role in targeting a younger audience by significantly increasing engagement and immersion. Element like rewards and challenges align with the preferences of younger users and will therefore make the visualisation more appealing.
  • While it may be effective for a younger audience, it may not be as appropriate for seniors as it can potentially overwhelm them due to the dynamic and interactive aspect of gamification
  • As a motivational tool, gamification plays an essential role in encouraging users to explore the visualisation with intent. By containing rewards and challenges, it creates a sense of purpose, turning the traditional data exploration process into a more enjoyable and rewarding experience.
  • By actively incorporating gamification elements, the visualisation has the ability to increase information recall due to the increased intent when exploring information.
Visual Engagement
  • Elements such as appealing visuals, interactive graphics, colour scheme, and multimedia contribute towards a more captivating user experience keeping users engaged with the content.
  • Visual stimuli plays a significant role in increasing memory retention as it creates key moments where the information presented becomes more memorable and thus likely to be retained by users.
Social Media Integration
  • Social media integration enables users to share interesting findings or visualisations with a broader audience, Increasing the reach of the content.
  • Additionally, social media integration facilitates collaboration by providing an additional means for user discussion. Users can engage in conversation, share insights, and collaborate on their interpretation of the visualised data, enhancing the collaborative aspect of the visualisation.
Credibility Source
  • Attaching a credible source to the visualisation increases the trust and confidence placed behind the presented information.
  • Reduces the anxiety associated with trusting foreign information by having a name attached whilst also building digital literacy
Responsiveness
  • Responsiveness of annotations may vary across devices, impacting the user experience. While higher-end devices with greater processing power may display smooth interactions, lower-end equipment, such as slow internet speed or older generation devices, may result in poor immersion and potential disassociation from the content due to lack of responsive behaviour.
  • On the other hand, a high level of responsiveness contributes to increased immersion. As smooth interactions and quick loading times create user feedback, allowing users to engage more fully with the content, creating a sense of connection and immersion.
Focus on Data or Storytelling
  • Annotations involving more data-rich content may be more appealing to younger adults as the depth of information and complexity may align more with their preferences and requirements for detailed analysis and exploration.
  • Storytelling, on the other hand, contains a broader appeal, capturing all audiences due to its reduced cognitive load from a linear and guided experience. In doing so, more associations with experiences is created, making the content more accessible and engaging across different age groups.
Positioning
  • Annotations should appear close to the content they describe as it creates greater association between the visualisation and the additional information
  • Utilising visual cues such as arrows or lines to connect annotations with their corresponding content enhances the clarity of the information and therefore helps users easily follow the association between the annotation and the relevant visualisation.
  • Ensuring that annotations do not obstruct or cover parts of the visualisation or content plays an essential role in creating a clear association between objects and allows for a greater comprehensive understanding of the information presented.
  • Visual hierarchy plays an essential role in reducing visual clutter as it involves placing the most important information in the foreground and less crucial details in the background, preferably with a subtle blur effect. In doing so, it not only directs the users attention, but also aids in maintaining an organised narrative visualisation.
Annotation Duration
  • Aspects where annotation duration plays a role is displaying data for a limited amount of time, it is important to strike the right balance in duration. If the duration is too short, users may miss important details that may lead to potential gaps in comprehension. Conversely, if the duration is too long, it may become distracting and may hinder the overall user experience.
Collapisble Annotation
  • Collapsible annotations enable for a clean and intuitive way to reduce the amount of clutter on the screen as it enables the user the flexibility to display more content based on the level of detail or knowledge required.
Visual Cues
  • Visual cues provide guidance, helping users understand interactive elements, navigate the interface, and interpret the content effectively
  • Contributes to an enhanced user experience by making the interface more intuitive and user-friendly

Conclusion

This work has recommended annotation usage for narrative visualization.
